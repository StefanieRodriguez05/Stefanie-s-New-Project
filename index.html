Ending World Hunger

In the US alone, over 34 million people suffer from starvation and food insecurity. Find out how to make a change today and help those in need.

Effects of being hungry?

People often do not notice the multitude of negative consequences caused by world hunger. For example, when students are hungry, they cannot focus in school. Consequentially, there is a drop in their performance and overall siccess i. school. 50% of children facing hunger need to repeat a grade. Additionally, hunger increases one's risk of chronic disease. According to the USDA, there is a strong connection between hunger and chronic diseases like high blood pressure, heart disease, and diabetes. In fact, 58% of the households that receive food from the Feeding America network have one member with high blood pressure. And 33% have a member with diabetes.

Ways to help

Poverty and Inequality are the root causes of world hunger. By donating to charities that target food insecurity, one can help provide those in need with the funds necessary to procure their daily sustenance. Additionally, planting community gardens in areas that suffer major food insecurity is another way to provide people with a healthy, free food source. Reducing food waste is another great way to stop food insecurity by outting the food that would have gone to waste to better use.
